Consult or update the paper narrative: the paper's argument, claims, and rationale. $ARGUMENTS

`paper_narrative.md` is the curated story of the paper and the least forgiving destination, so
this is the only way it is edited and it **never writes a single word without your explicit,
sentence-by-sentence approval**. Default to advising, not writing. Research analog of
`/mem-canon`. The narrative is canonical only for the *synthesis* (the argument, cross-experiment
tables, which claim each result backs); per-run facts live in the experiment detail leaves, and
the narrative links down to them.

**Always advise first.** Whatever is asked, reason before touching the file:
- Read `paper_narrative.md`, `experiments_important.md`, and the relevant detail leaf/leaves
  under `experiments/` that the claim rests on (and the technical notes for methodology if
  needed).
- Say where the thing belongs — one of **TL;DR (abstract) · Method · Main results · Ablation ·
  Supplementary · Open/pending · Ignore** — with a one-line rationale. Flag any conflict with an
  existing claim. Ask a follow-up if the call is genuinely unclear.
- Stop there. Do not write unless I explicitly tell you to make the change.

**Placement gating.** Editing an existing claim and adding a new one are both high gate. Keep the
narrative skimmable and in paper order; never restructure it on your own.

**Write only after I approve a concrete change, sentence by sentence:**
- Pull every fact and number from the **detail leaf** (the source of truth), never from memory, a
  summary, or the headline line; never invent or paraphrase a figure.
- Draft a compact block in the right section: the claim or result, its headline number, and an
  evidence link down to the detail leaf (plus a code anchor where relevant). Match the terse,
  pointer style; no path dumps, no restated methodology.
- Walk it one line at a time: show each sentence, get my approval, write only the lines I
  approve. If I reject or reword a line, use my version.

**Refresh.** `paper_narrative.md` is not regenerated by `/paper-index`; the only update is this
approved, hand-written edit. If the change also implies a run should join or leave the important
subset, say so and let `/logexp` flip that leaf's `**Important:**` flag — do not edit the index
here.

**Always:** detail leaves are the source of truth and the narrative links down to them; if you
spot something addable, inconsistent, or stale while reading, suggest it, never write it
unprompted. When in doubt, do not write.
