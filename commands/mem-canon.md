Consult or update the canon: the project story, key decisions, and rationale. $ARGUMENTS

`canon/` is the most important and least forgiving store, so this is the only way it is edited
and it **never writes a single word without your explicit, line-by-line approval**. Default to
advising, not writing.

**Always advise first.** Whatever is asked, reason before touching any file:
- Read the relevant `canon/` doc(s), `canon.md`, and the source-of-truth leaves under
  `journal/` and `knowledge/` that the claim rests on.
- Say where the thing belongs: an existing decision doc, `north-star.md`, or a new canon doc
  (e.g. a new numbered decision), with a one-line rationale. Flag any conflict with an existing
  canon statement.
- Stop there. Do not write unless I explicitly tell you to make the change.

**Placement gating.** Editing an existing canon doc and creating a new one are both high gate.
For a new doc, propose the path and title (e.g. `canon/decisions/0003-<slug>.md`) and get an OK
before creating it. Never restructure `canon/` on your own.

**Write only after I approve a concrete change, sentence by sentence:**
- Pull every fact and number from the source leaf (the truth), never from memory or a summary;
  never invent or paraphrase a figure.
- Draft a compact block in the right place: the claim or decision, its rationale, and an
  evidence link down to the leaf. Match the terse, pointer style; no path dumps, no restated
  methodology.
- Walk it one line at a time: show each sentence, get my approval, write only the lines I
  approve. If I reject or reword a line, use my version.

**Refresh the index.** After an approved write, run `/mem-index canon`.

**Always:** leaves are the source of truth and canon links down to them; if you spot something
addable, inconsistent, or stale while reading, suggest it, never write it unprompted. When in
doubt, do not write.
